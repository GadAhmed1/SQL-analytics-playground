Social Media Addiction Analysis – Key KPIs & Insights
====================================================

1. Total Students
- Total number of students in the dataset: 705

2. Average Daily Social Media Usage
- Average daily usage: 4.92 hours per day

3. Students Distribution by Country
- Top countries by number of students:
  - India:  7.52%
  - USA:    5.67%
  - Canada: 4.82%

4. Students Distribution by Gender
- Female: 50.07%
- Male:   49.93%

5. Students Distribution by Academic Level
- Undergraduate: 50.07%
- Graduate:      46.10%
- High School:   3.83%

6. Most Used Social Media Platform
- Most popular platform: Instagram
- Usage percentage: 35.32%

7. Usage vs Mental Health
- There is a negative correlation between
  daily usage hours and mental health score.
- Higher usage is associated with worse mental health.

8. Sleep Duration by Usage Level
- Low usage: AVG 7.77 hours of sleep
- Medium usage: AVG 7.99 hours of sleep
- High usage: AVG 6.63 hours of sleep
- Very High usage: AVG 5.54 hours of sleep

9. Academic Performance Impact
- Percentage of students reporting negative academic impact: 64.25%

10. Top 5 Countries by Average Addiction Score
1. Armenia – Avg score: 9
2. USA – Avg score: 8.6
3. UAE – Avg score: 8.125
4. Andorra – Avg score: 8
5. India – Avg score: 7.50

11. Addiction Score by Academic Level
- High School: Avg score 8.04
- Graduate: Avg score 6.24
- Undergraduate: Avg score 6.49

12. High Usage & Poor Sleep
- Percentage of students using social media > 5 hours/day
  and sleeping < 6 hours/night: 18.58%

13. TikTok vs Instagram Addiction
- TikTok users average addiction score: 7.429
- Instagram users average addiction score: 6.554
- Conclusion: TikTok users are more addicted than Instagram users.

14. Addiction Score vs Social Conflicts
- There is a strong  relationship between
  addiction score and number of conflicts caused by social media.
- Higher addiction leads to more conflicts.

15. Student Segmentation (Usage & Addiction)
- Low usage:       0%
- Medium usage:    29%
- High usage:      55%
- Very High usage: 15%

----------------------------------------------------
Overall Insight:
Excessive social media usage is strongly linked to higher addiction
scores, poorer sleep quality, increased conflicts, and negative
academic performance.
