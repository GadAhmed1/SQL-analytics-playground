## Overview
The analysis shows a strong link between excessive social media usage and
higher addiction levels, poorer sleep quality, worse mental health,
and negative academic performance.

## Key KPIs
- **Total Students:** 705  
- **Average Daily Usage:** 4.92 hours/day  
- **Most Used Platform:** Instagram (35.32%)  
- **Negative Academic Impact:** 64.25% of students  

## Demographics
- **Gender Distribution:**
  - Female: 50.07%
  - Male: 49.93%

- **Academic Level Distribution:**
  - Undergraduate: 50.07%
  - Graduate: 46.10%
  - High School: 3.83%

- **Top Countries by Student Count:**
  - India: 7.52%
  - USA: 5.67%
  - Canada: 4.82%

## Mental Health & Sleep
- Higher daily usage is associated with **worse mental health scores**.
- Average sleep duration decreases as usage increases:
  - Low usage: 7.77 hours
  - Medium usage: 7.99 hours
  - High usage: 6.63 hours
  - Very high usage: 5.54 hours
- **18.58%** of students use social media more than 5 hours/day and sleep less than 6 hours.

## Addiction Insights
- TikTok users have higher average addiction scores than Instagram users.
- High school students show the **highest average addiction score**.
- Higher addiction scores are strongly linked to more social conflicts.

## Conclusion
Excessive social media usage significantly increases addiction risk and
negatively affects sleep, mental health, academic performance,
and social relationships.
